Robin Cantrell lives and works in New York City, currently as a dancer with Battery Dance Company and Rebecca Kelly Ballet. She creates site specific dance for film with local video and sound artists in locations throughout New York, including works set on the Brooklyn Bridge and in China Town. Robin holds a B.F.A. in Dance Performance and a degree in Social Anthropology from the University of Utah where she attended school as a William F. Christensen Scholarship recipient. She danced professionally for several years with City Ballet of San Diego where she performed lead roles in "Concerto Borocco" and "Leave the Light on," a contemporary pas de deux created for her. Her choreography has been showcased in Salt Lake City's Mariott Center for Dance and Kingsbury Hall. Robin has participated in programs with Malashock Dance Company, Repertory Dance Theatre, Complexions, and the Martha Graham Company.

Mira Cook is a choreographer and dancer living in San Francisco. With over twenty years of training in classical ballet and contemporary movement, Mira has danced and created choreography for companies such as: City Ballet of San Diego, the Austin Dance Ensemble and SUSHI Performance and Visual Arts. Mira has enjoyed dancing lead roles in Balanchine's "Rubies," City Ballet's "The Rite of Spring," and Double Vision's "Thicket." She has participated in programs at Ballet Austin, Boston Ballet, LINES Ballet, ODC Dance, and Kunst-Stoff. Mira currently performs with Double Vision Dance and Intermedia, Capacitor, Brooke Broussard and Liss Fain Dance in San Francisco. She also writes and records music from home.
